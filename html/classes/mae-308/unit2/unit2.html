Fluid Statics

Introduction

Fluid Statics is the analysis of fluids when there is no shearing stress in the fluid and the only forces that develop on surfaces are due to gravity and pressure.

Pressure at a Point

Pressure at a Point is the pressure taken at an individual particle or location.
  • p = ρgh

Pascal's Law says that if a fluid is at rest the pressure is independent of the direction so long as there is no shearing stress in the fluid.

Equation for Pressure Field

Pressure varies from point to point
  • Surface forces due to pressure vary as based on the depth of the liquid
  • If we assume that the pressure at the center of a cube is equal to P, then...
    • ∑Fx = - dP ⁄ dx = ρax
    • ∑Fy = - dP ⁄ dy = ρay
    • ∑Fz = - dP ⁄ dz - γ = ρaz
  • If the fluid and the object are at rest, then p = ρgh
General Equation of Motion: -∇p = γk̂ = ρa


Pressure Variation in a Fluid

For Incompressible Fluids:
  • p1 = γh + p2 (hydrostatic distribution)
  • h = p1 - p2 ⁄ γ (pressure head)
For Compressible Fluids:
  • p1 = γh + p2 (hydrostatic distribution)
  • ln(p2 ⁄ p1) = -g⁄R * ∫z1z2 (dz ⁄ T)
  • For an isothermal process, p2 = p1e(-g(z2 - z1) ⁄ RT0)

Measurements of Pressure

Pressure at a point is reported in one of two ways
  • Absolute Pressure is the pressure measured relative to a vacuum.
  • Gage Pressure is the pressure measured relative to the local atmospheric pressure.
Atmospheric Pressure is typically measured using a mercury barometer.
  • patm = γh + pvapor
    • pvapor = .16 Pa and can typically be ignored.

Pressure Measurement Devices

Manometers are pressure devices that measure pressure using the height of liquid columns. There are three common types:
  • Piezometer are bulbs with a vertical tube coming out of their side.
    • pa = γ1h1
  • U-tube manometer are bulbs with a u shape tube that drops down after coming out of their side.
    • pa = γ2h2 - γ1h1 + patm
  • Inclined tube manometer have two bulbs with a raised incline tube connecting them.
    • pa - pb = γ2l2sin(θ)


Linear Motion

Container of liquid translating along a straight line with a constant acceleration (a).
  • dz⁄dy = - ay ⁄g + az
  • No acceleration in the x-direction (ax = 0).
  • Pressure constant along the free surface.
  • All lines of constant pressure parallel to the free surface.

Rigid Body Rotation

Fluid rotating with a constant angular velocity (ω) about an axis.
  • ar = rω2
  • For a rigid-body rotation, surfaces of constant pressure are parabolic.
  • z = ω2r2⁄2g

Hydrostatic Force on a Plane Surface

For a submerged surface, forces develop due to the fluid.
  • px, net = .5ρgh = γh
    • Acts 1⁄3 of the way from the bottom
  • py, net = ρgh = γh
  • Pressure varies linearly with depth (incompressible fluid).

Hydrostatic Force on a Plane Surface

Consider a curved portion of the bottom of a container
  • FH and FV are the components of force the tank exerts on the fluid.
    • FV is equal to the sum of the weight of the fluid above the surface and the force on the plane area formed by the projection on horizontal plane.
    • FH is equal to the force on the plane area formed by the projection on vertical plane.
