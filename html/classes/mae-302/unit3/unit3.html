Refrigeration cycles

Refrigeration Cycles

The purpose of a refrigeration cycle is to...
  • Remove heat from a cold space (Refrigerator)
  • Reject heat from a warm space (Heat pump)

The required input and desired outputs...
  • Wnet, in is the input.
  • QL and QH is the output.
  • Thus, QL + Wnet, in = QH
  • QH > QL and QH > Wnet, in

Coefficient of Performance:
  • COPR = QL ⁄ Wnet, in
  • COPH = QH ⁄ Wnet, in
  • COPH = COPR + 1


Ideal Vapor-Compression Refrigeration Cycle

Process 1 → 2:
  • Isentropic compression in a compressor
  • Ẇc = ṁ1(h2 - h1)
  • wc = h2 - h1
  • For a non-ideal compressor
    • nc = Wc, s ⁄ wc = h2, s - h1 ⁄ h2 - h1
    • h2 = h1 + h2, s - h1 ⁄ nc

Process 2 → 3:
  • Constant pressure heat rejection in a condenser
  • Q̇H = ṁ(h2 - h3)
  • qH = h2 - h3

Process 3 → 4:
  • Throttling in an expansion device
  • h3 = h4
  • It may be desirable to subcool the refrigerant at state 3 such that the outlet of state 3 is a compressed liquid at P3. This expands the path for heat removal and heat input

Process 4 → 1:
  • Constant pressure heat absorption in an evaporator
  • Q̇L = ṁ(h1 - h4)
  • qL = h1 - h4


Innovative Vapor-Compression Refrigeration Systems

Innovative Vapor-Compression Refrigeration Systems:
  • The ordinary refrigeration cycle is the most widely used cycle due to its simple, reliable, and inexpensive nature.
  • When required temperatures for the refrigerator are very low one the following cycles may be used...
    • Cascade refrigeration systems
    • Multistage compression refrigeration systems
    • Multipurpose refrigeration systems with a single compressor
    • Liquefaction of gases

Cascade Refrigeration Systems:
  • Allows for different refrigerants.
  • Used when lower temperatures are required and the temperature range may be to large for a single vapor-compression cycle.
  • Consists of multiple cycles linked together by connecting the evaporator of one with the compressor of another.
  • ṁA(h5 - h8) = ṁB(h2 - h3)
  • COPR, cascade = ṁB(h1 - h4) ⁄ ṁA(h6 - h5) + ṁB(h2 - h1)

Multistage Compression Refrigeration Systems:
  • When the cascade refrigerants are the same, the connecting compressor and evaporator can be replaced by a single flash chamber.
  • p2 = p3 = p9
  • ṁh9 = X6ṁh3 + (1 - X6)ṁh2 or h9 = Xgh3 + (1 - X6)h2
  • Ẇc = (1 - X6)ṁ(h2 - h1) + ṁ(h4 - h9)
  • Q̇L = (1 - X6)ṁ(h1 - h8)
  • Q̇H = ṁ(h4 - h5)

Multipurpose Refrigeration Systems with a Single Compressor:
  • Used when multiple temperatures need to be maintained at the same time such as in a refigerator/freezer combo.
  • Routes all exit streams from the evaporators to a single compressor and allow that compressor to handle all compressor processes.

Liquefaction of Gases:
  • Allows for cryogenic temperatures (< 100 K) in order to liquefy gases. These gases mainly include liquid Nitrogen and Oxygen.
