Ideal Gas Mixtures and Psychrometrics

Ideal Gas Mixtures

Ideal Gases:
  • Defined by 2 main governing equations. These equations being pv = RT and pV = mRT = NRT where...
    • m is the mass
    • N is the number of moles
    • R is the universal gas constant.
    • R is the gas constant.
  • Enthalpy and internal energy are functions of temperature only.

For Ideal Gases Mixtures:
  • Masses of k different components add up.
  • Moles of k different components add up.
  • Volume occupied by k different components are shared.
  • Temperatures are shared.
  • Pressures contributed by each component add up to the total pressure.

Mole Fractions:
  • Yi = Ni ⁄ N

Mass Fractions:
  • mfi = mi ⁄ m

Sanity Checks:
  • 0 ≤ Yi ≤ 1 and 0 ≤ mfi ≤ 1

Mixture Molar Mass:
  • Mi = mi ⁄ Ni

Contribution to Total Pressure:
  • pi = Yip
  • The fraction of the total pressure contributed by species i is the mole fraction.

When does the Ideal Gas Assumption Apply?
  • General form with the compressibility factor, Z.
  • pv = ZRT
  • Z = Z(T ⁄ Tcr, p ⁄ pcr)
