One-Dimensional, Steady-State Conduction without Thermal Energy Generation

Conduction Analysis

Methodology:
  1. Specify appropriate form of the heat equation.
  2. Solve for the temperature distribution.
  3. Apply Fourier’s law to determine the heat flux.

Common Geometries:
  • The Plane Wall: Described in rectangular (x) coordinate. Area perpendicular to direction of heat transfer is constant (independent of x).
    • Temperature difference for a constant k: T(x) = Ts, 1 + (Ts, 2 - Ts, 1)x⁄L
    • Heat Transfer Rate: qx'' = -kAdT⁄dx = KA⁄L(Ts, 1 - Ts, 2)
    • Overall Heat Transfer Coefficient (U): qx = UA∆Toverall
    • Rtot = 1⁄UA
  • The Tube Wall: Radial conduction through tube wall.
    • Heat Equation: 1⁄r * d⁄dr * (kr * dt⁄dr) = 0
    • Temperature Distribution: T(r) = (Ts,1 - Ts,2)⁄ln(r1 / r2) * ln(r⁄r2) + Ts, 2
    • Heat Flux and Heat Rate:
      • qr'' = -k * dT⁄dr = k⁄(rln(r2/r1)) * (Ts,1 - Ts,2)
      • qr' = 2π rqr'' = 2π k⁄(ln(r2/r1)) * (Ts,1 - Ts,2)
      • qr = 2π rLqr'' = 2π Lk⁄(ln(r2/r1)) * (Ts,1 - Ts,2)
    • Conduction Resistance:
      • Rt,cond = ln(r2/r1)⁄2π Lk
      • R't,cond = ln(r2/r1)⁄2π k
  • The Spherical Shell: Radial conduction through shell wall.
    • Heat Equation: 1⁄r2 * d⁄dr * (r2 * dt⁄dr) = 0
    • Temperature Distribution: T(r) = Ts,1 - (Ts,1 - Ts,2) * 1 - (r1/r)⁄1 - (r1/r2)
    • Heat Flux and Heat Rate:
      • qr'' = -k * dT⁄dr = k⁄r2[(1/r1) - (1/r2)] * (Ts,1 - Ts,2)
      • qr = 4π r2qr'' = 4π k⁄(1/r1) - (1/r2) * (Ts,1 - Ts,2) = ΔToverall⁄Rtot = UAΔToverall
    • Thermal Resistance:
      • Rt,cond = (1/r1) - (1/r2)⁄4π k
